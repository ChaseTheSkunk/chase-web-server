    Name: Tage
    Pronunciation: TAH-gay or TAH-gee
    Scientific Name: Catalinursa lanata
    Classification: Feral
    Height: 10-24" (0.3-0.6 m)
    Threat Level: Low
    Associated Elements: None

    Etymology: From an old Eastern language, watage, meaning "fluff" or from the common word doggy.

    Characteristics: Tages come in a variety of sizes, colors, and breeds. Large tages often don't reach 2 feet in height while smaller breeds can be under a foot in height. Their colors come in a wide variety from brown to black, gray, and white. Tages are known for their thick, soft fur which groups up in bunches around their cheeks. They also have long, pointed ears; however, the ears of all breeds are floppy and do not stand up straight. Tages also have short muzzles. They have long wispy tails that can stand upright.

    Habitat: Tages are primarily domestic creatures and live in anthro homes. Others can be found in urban areas. On rare occasions they are roaming in the countryside.

    Behavior: Tages are mostly domesticated, making them very docile and loyal creatures. Their gait appears to be rather clumsy at times. The heavier their build, the more clumsy they look but also the more appealing they are to prospective owners. They have a keen sense of smell and hearing, often relying more on them then their sense of sight which tends to be rather poor. Some breeds have a mild trashy odor to them unless cleaned and groomed often. Tages will eat a mix of meat and grains. Their diets don't need to be watched very closely, however, as they are capable of digesting a wide variety of foods. Stray tages will often rummage through garbage cans for this very reason. Beef and venison tend to be their favorite meats while natural sugars and starches are also favored.

    Interactions: Tages are most commonly kept as pets. Larger breeds are used for guarding territory or for hunting purposes.
