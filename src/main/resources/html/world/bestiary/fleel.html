    Name: Fleel
    Pronunciation: FLEEL
    Scientific Name: Anguilla plesiopterygia
    Classification: Feral
    Height: ~1' (~0.3 m)
    Threat Level: Low
    Associated Elements: None

    Etymology: From the Common language, flipper and eel.

    Characteristics: Fleels bear a resemblance to eels and are classified as such. They tend to have smooth skin that is green or close to green in color, spotted markings down the lengths of their back, and yellow undersides. On the tips of their tails are usually colorful dorsal fins. Two key traits separate them from normal eels, nonetheless. The more obvious of the two is their advanced pectoral fins. They look like and act functionally equivalent to flippers. In fact, most anthros mistake them for flippers and have named them based on this specific characteristic throughout history. The other trait that separates them from eels is the curved barbs on their heads. These barbs secrete a poison that, in a large enough dose, can paralyze another creature.

    Habitat: Fleels tend to live in the oceans of Casarok. They can most commonly be found away from the coastline.

    Behavior: Fleels spend their entire lives in the ocean. They are carnivorous creatures and will hunt smaller prey using the poisonous barbs on their heads. To do this, they use their advanced fins and sleek bodies to move quickly and agilely through the water. They ram their prey with their heads to either pierce them or spread their poison through contact. A small dose of the poison will paralyze potential prey. Larger doses are needed to stop larger creatures, so they prefer not to attack or hunt larger creatures. Instead they will use their speed and mobility to escape danger. Thus, typically, they server little to no threat to anthros who go near them.

    Interactions: Fleels are fished and consumed by many seaside anthros in the world, though some inland seafood restaurants have fleels shipped to them for others to enjoy. They are often cooked without the head where most of the poison is stored. Fleel "flippers" are even considered a delicacy by some people.
