    Name: Drekaldr
    Other Names: Frost Dragon
    Pronunciation: dreh-KAL-der
    Scientific Name: Draco frigoris
    Race: Draconic
    Height: 4-5' (1.2-1.5 m)
    Associated Elements:

    Etymology: From Drekaldrish, dreki meaning "dragon" and kaldr meaning "cold".

    Characteristics: Drekaldar are known for their primarily white scales which look almost like pure snow. Light blue scales travel along their snout, neck, front of the torso, and underside. Blue fur covers the tops of their heads and runs down the length of their back all the way to the tips of their tails. Their heads feature two long, straight, deep blue horns that resemble icicles. The snout bears a small horn at the very tip as well as large nostrils on either side of the horn. Most drekaldar are heavy set and have a thick layer of fat around their abdominal region. Only their warriors are slimmed down so as to keep in shape for battle. The feet and hands are equipped with short but sharp claws that are capable of picking through ice if needed. Drekaldr wings are small and light, making them incapable of flight; however, they can fold these wings in a particular way on their backs to act like a fin. Along with their natural buoyancy and their thick, muscular tails, they are adept at swimming through frigid waters. The average lifespan of a drekaldr is 250 years.

    Abilities: Drekaldar have frost breath as their primary weapon, able to chill their opponents. They also wield potent frost magic and are easily capable of freezing others into solid blocks of ice if the situation calls for it. Their magical kit also includes the ability to breathe underwater. They have a tolerance for extreme cold and can survive in temperatures much lower than any other creature in Casarok. Drekaldar are also extremely intelligent, making them able to learn new things at an accelerated rate or strategize quickly on the battlefield.

    Habitat: Drekaldar live in the polar regions of Casarok. They typically remain reclusive and will live underground or underwater where other creatures would be hard-pressed to find them.

    Behavior: Drekaldar are rational thinkers. They prioritize the pursuit of knowledge over barbarism and fighting whenever possible. Their academic teachings are long sought after, and the greatest Anthro universities in the world seek to employ the few drekaldar that leave their homes. Their libraries are sought after as well, including the fabled Great Library that is said to exist in the Frostlands. However, they tend to remain very reclusive and few drekaldar ever set foot too far outside their place of origin. Because of their keen intellect and small numbers, their government is very well organized and crime is at a minimum in drekaldr villages. During the long winter months some drekaldar hibernate to reduce the need for food when it is at its most scarce.

    Diet: The drekaldar are primarily petravores, meaning they take in nutrients and minerals from the consumption of rocks. They will construct traps for prey ferals and monsters to use for meat.

    Language: The primary language historically was Old Norse, which evolved into Drekaldrish over time. Usually only those drekaldar that venture out from their homes learn Common, but there can be one or two Common translators depending on the village. Due to their knack for picking up new information quickly, they do learn other languages as necessary.
