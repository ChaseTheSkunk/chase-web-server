    Name: Alepod
    Other Names: Vivirga (Dracammarum)
    Pronunciation: AL-uh-pod
    Scientific Name: Floradon praetrepidans
    Classification: Floradon
    Height: 3-6" (0.08-0.15 m)
    Threat Level: Low
    Associated Elements:

    Etymology: From the Common words alert and pod. Formerly known as Vivirga from the Dracammarum words vigilate meaning "vigilant" and virga meaning "sprout".

    Characteristics: Alepods come in a variety of colors and shapes, depending on the biome they come from. Regardless of their location, they all share some similar qualities. The main body is spherical or ovoid in shape. A thick but flexible stem allows them to wiggle and lean in various directions and acts as their main method of motion. They all have three leafy protrusions at the base of the stem, which seem to act like armlets. All alepods are rather small at a half foot at their tallest.

    Habitat: Alepods live in a variety of habitats. They are primarily found in forests, meadows, and swamops.

    Behavior: Alepods usually grow in patches with dozens of other alepods of the same type. They remain rooted in that same spot their entire lives. The sprout-like creatures are very skittish and weary of predators. If they spot a potential threat, they let out a cry, pheromones, or both in order to attract other floradons to protect them. Larger patches of alepods are generally more successful in summoning other floradons to their aid. Without the aid of more powerful floradons, however, alepods are rather defenseless. They will bite at their attackers even though these efforts are often futile. Alepods more commonly bite at insects that pass by though; they consist themselves mostly off of photosynthesis, but they are capable of metabolizing small bugs and spiders for their sustenance as well.

    Interactions: Most anthros will avoid alepods if they are encountered in the wild due to their ability to attract more powerful creatures to them. Nevertheless, some hunters will use this ability to their advantage. Alepods can be farmed for food as a plant-based protein source. They are also bred for use as pets, usually kept like potted plants (as pictured, center) near a window or some other light source or outdoors. It is usually imperative for farmers and breeders of alepods to have some form of magical ward in the area to prevent the sprout-like creatures from summoning other floradons to the area.
