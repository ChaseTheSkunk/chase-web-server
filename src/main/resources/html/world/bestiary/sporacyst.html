    Name: Sporacyst
    Pronunciation: SPORE-uh-sist
    Scientific Name: Floradon sporacystis
    Classification: Floradon
    Height: 3-4' (0.8-1.2 m)
    Threat Level: High
    Associated Elements:

    Etymology: From the Common language words spore and cyst. The root word cyst refers to how it encapsulates the organism affected by its spores. The word has its roots in Dracammarum.

    Characteristics: Sporacysts are large green creatures in the general shape of an anthro. The shape of the head, ears, and snout differ heavily between different individuals; however, they all have vine-like tendrils spreading outward from the tops of their muzzles that drape over their mouths like curtains. They have a hulking appearance to them with the top of the back protruding out over top the head. This hump on their backs is always covered in moss. Often, various types of fungi will sprout from this mossy covering. Vines or leaf fronds are also attached to their bodies in a multitude of places. The arms and legs are thick and muscular. The digits on the hands end in sharp claw-like protrusions while the feet take on the general shape of roots.

    Habitat: Sporacysts live primarily in jungles and swamps. They have been known to live in forests, however. Villages in the past have been overrun with these creatures as well.

    Behavior: Sporacysts are slow, lumbering creatures. They are almost entirely mindless with their only will being to spread their spores to other creatures. These spores are their method of reproduction. When inhaled or absorbed through an open wound, they infect the host. The host then develops cold-like symptoms within a day or two. After another few days, they can develop green splotches on their scales or fur. This discoloration can be difficult to detect for some anthros who already have green skin or fur like certain dragons or cu siths. If the spores are not detected and treated before this time, they will attack the brain of the host and start taking over the central nervous system of the host. Twitching, convulsions, and brain damage are common at this stage of the disease. Finally, the spores kill and take over the host as they become in a sort of zombified state. The body changes rapidly after this stage, becoming stronger and growing plant-based appendages, until they turn into the known image of a sporacyst like the one pictured above.

    Interactions: Sporacysts are considered highly threatening and are often avoided when possible. They are hunted out of necessity most often. Nonetheless, the mushrooms that grow on their backs are usually those with medicinal or alchemical properties. The fungi are often harvested when a sporacyst is hunted down. Because of the disease they cause with their spores, special facial masks have been developed to prevent their inhalation. Healers and doctors in areas where the threat of sporacyst contact is high are required to know magic or medicine that can counteract the disease brought on by the spores.
