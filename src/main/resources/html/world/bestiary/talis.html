    Name: Talis
    Pronunciation: TAL-iss
    Scientific Name: Aeonarris spiritalis
    Race: Anthro
    Height: 3-4' (0.8-1.2 m)
    Associated Elements:

    Etymology: From Dracammarum, short for spiritalis meaning "spiritual". They are also known as the Jīngshén de rén -- meaning "Spiritual ones" -- in the east.

    Characteristics: Talis are known primarily for their light purple fur. The actual hue of their fur does not vary much between individuals. The fur is very thick and fluffy around their necks, another distinguishing feature of their forms. The heads of talis generally have large, curved ears and short muzzles with small nosepads. Their eyes differ in color among each other, but they are all deeply colored. The tails of the talis are long and thin with a large tuft of lighter colored fur at the end. The tuft is tipped white somewhat like that of a fox.

    Abilities: The ears of the talis give them acute hearing. They can hear the slightest or the most distant noises. Some have said that they can even hear the words of ghosts and spirits in the world, though this has long been debunked as a myth. In addition, the talis have a fine sense of touch on the tips of their tails. They become alert when someone or something brushes against their tail tufts. The talis are also known to have an affinity for learning spirit magics, although not all talis even learn from the metaphysical school or pick up magic of any kind at all.

    Habitat: Talis live mostly in the temperate climates of Casarok. They are more commonly found in the western and eastern portions of the world, however. Some talis can be found in the northern mountains as well, yet they do not cross into the Snowscape.

    Behavior: The talis tend to live among other Anthros and pick up their local culture. Regardless of the place they call home, talis tend to have great respect for those who are learned in the ways of spirit magic. In the rare case that the talis create a community amongst themselves, this aspect of their character is enhanced. The leader of a pure talis village is usually the greatest spirit wizard. In fact, the hierarchy of some talis villages is determined by knowledge of spirit magic. The talis of such communities are so attuned to spirit magic that it is said their magical teachings rival those of even the dmuun'dre. Runes and symbols for spirit magic are popular and adorn much of their clothing and weaponry. Talis that are not part of these communities will sometimes make pilgrimages to these places. It is said to be a soul-cleansing experience, especially for those of other species who decide to make the trek.

    Diet: Talis diet differs depending on the region they are from; however, they are omnivorous like much of the other Anthro race and eat a wide diversity of food.

    Language: The talis speak a variety of languages, depending on region. In more modern times, they speak the Common language.
