    Name: Ettette
    Pronunciation: ET-tet
    Scientific Name: Cerveralus domesticus
    Classification: Feral
    Height: 12-24" (0.3-0.6 m)
    Threat Level: Very Low
    Associated Elements: None

    Etymology: From an old Western language, either attuck or otuhk meaning "deer" and the Common ending -ette meaning "little".

    Characteristics: Ettettes are very deer-like in appearance. They have caramel-colored fur with yellow or white markings around their neck and abdomen. Some ettettes also bear this cream-colored fur around their eyes and muzzles. Some also have soft patterns of spots lining their flanks. They differ from anthro deer in several ways, however. The most noticeable is their lack of antlers; instead, they don small nubs on their heads where antlers would grow on other cervine creatures. Their nosepad is significantly smaller as well. The tails tend to be fluffier and have greater range of motion as well.

    Habitat: Ettettes have become domesticated, so they live in anthro homes. Those that are wild can often be found in wooded areas.

    Behavior: Ettettes are very popular pets in the western and central regions of Casarok. They were originally domesticated to aid in foraging for edible plants and herbs. Some anthros do still use ettettes for gathering herbs nevertheless. Ettettes can be rather skittish and easily frightened. If they hear a threat with their large ears, their first instinct is to turn tail and run. Their diets consist mostly of plants, fungi, and simple carbohydrates. They have little need for grooming due to their short fur, but their hooves require regular upkeep.

    Interactions: Ettettes are most commonly kept as pets. While their earlier uses included foraging for berries and herbs, today, they are more often used for companionship and defending gardens against weeds and other invaders.
