    Name: Terraboldt
    Pronunciation: tear-uh-BOLD
    Scientific Name: Aldabrachelys forctis
    Classification: Monster
    Height: 4-6' (1.2-1.8 m)
    Threat Level: Moderate
    Associated Elements:

    Etymology: From the Common words terrapin and boulder.

    Characteristics: Terraboldts are similar to giant tortoises in appearance. They have green or brown scaly skin on their heads, limbs, and tail. A large shell covers the torsos of these monsters. They are set apart from tortoises, however, by the composition of their shells. While the base composition of the shell is bone, the outer covering is fused with rock plating and shards. This attribute tends to make the shell jagged and bumpy rather than smooth. It also makes the shell extremely resistant to damage.

    Habitat: Terraboldts live in mountains and caverns. Due to their often slow nature, a terraboldt is never seen outside of these regions unless captured and brought somewhere by an anthro.

    Behavior: Terraboldts have a tendency to be calm and sluggish. They are strictly petravorous, meaning they only eat rocks to obtain the essential minerals they need to live. Thus, they do not hunt. They are often not alarmed by others coming by and will not fight unless they feel ' truly threatened. When becoming aggressive, terraboldts have the ability to call upon powerful earth magics. They can charge at enemies at surprisingly high speeds by moving the very ground beneath them. It is a common approach for these monsters to retreat into their shells and roll at an attacker like a boulder. They can also fire off shards of rocks from their shells like large bullets. These abilities are only available to terraboldts once they are of age. Early in the life of a terraboldt, they have softer, bony shells. By rolling on the ground and consuming rocks, they build up the toughness of their shells and their earth magic abilities over time. Until they are ready to defend themselves, they are under the close protection of their parents.

    Interactions: Terraboldts are often left alone by travellers and adventurers. They aren't particularly dangerous unless threatened, and they typically don't provide any renown for killing them. The shells can be useful as a makeshift basin or cauldron and have been used as such throughout the early ears of Casarokan history.
