    Name: Dašungallu
    Other Names: Storm Dragon
    Pronunciation: DAH-shoon-gah-loo
    Scientific Name: Draco nimbosus
    Race: Draconic
    Height: 3.5-4.5' (1-1.3 m)
    Associated Elements:

    Etymology: From dašungallish, the language of the dašungallu, adad meaning "thunder" and šungallu meaning "dragon".

    Characteristics: Dašungallï have primarily yellow scales with slightly thicker blue scales across their front and undersides. Their head is marked by spikes growing out the sides of their faces and bright blue, serrated horns that look like lightning bolts when viewed at certain angles. On top of their heads are brightly colored hair ranging from yellow to green to blue, which is traditionally kept in a wild style. Dašungallï have no visible ears, making them look particularly reptilian like their primordial ancestors. The tail is long and flexible with a barbed, sharp point at the end. Rather than having wings on their backs, their wings are attached to their arms. Additionally, dašungallï have the longest wingspan of any Draconic species. Both their arms and feet have opposable digits, allowing them to grip objects with any of their limbs. The average lifespan of a dašungallu is around 300 years.

    Abilities: Dašungallï wield wind as their breath attack. They are capable of exhaling a large gust of wind to push back foes or disorient and knock down airborne opponents. This wind breath can also be used to create a bubble of air around them. Coupled with their natural gift for flight, dašungallï can use this air pocket to fly nimbly and at very high speeds. Magically, they are able to use both lightning and wind magics. With these powers combined they can summon storms to aid them in battle. Their spiked tail tips can be used not only as a focal point for these magics but also as a competent spear weapon to impale foes. In combat, they have a tendency to use deceptive tactics through their combined abilities to disorient, paralyze, and strike their opponents.

    Habitat: Dašungallï are generally more spread out in the world than their other Draconic counterparts. Their most common homes are at high altitudes like mountains and cloudlands. Some dašungallï prefer the prairies though where storms and lightning are prevalent.

    Behavior: It is customary for a dašungallu to cross their arms over their chest when not in flight, wrapping their wings over the fronts of their bodies. This gesture is particularly used when meeting those of high respect or regard. They gather in tribes and have a tribal-like structure to their government. Tribes of dašungallï often work together and align with one another. Most other races and species are treated with disdain. If they are approached by someone who is not seen as much of a threat, the dašungallï will tolerate, tease, or outright trick them in a jostling manner. If they are approached by a threat or a hated enemy like the dracammas, then they will react brutally and with much violence.

    Diet: Dašungallï are omnivorous, although they have a heavy preference toward meat. Hunting and farming are both popular methods of gathering food; however, hunting is more favorable for those that live in high altitudes. Their speedy fliying ability allows them to go great distances to hunt for their food.

    Language: Early in their history, dašungallï used Akkadian as their language of choice. The language eventually evolved into Dašungallish. Some of them adopted the Common language upon its inception. In modern times, they speak either Common or Dašungallish based on the tribe they come from.
