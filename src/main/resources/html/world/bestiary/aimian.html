    Name: Aimian
    Pronunciation: AYE-mee-en
    Scientific Name: Floradon aimi
    Classification: Floradon
    Height: ~5' (~1.5 m)
    Threat Level: High
    Associated Elements:

    Etymology: Named after the goddess Aimi, whose temple they were first spotted near.

    Characteristics: Aimians are large green plants with thick, pointed roots and flowing vines all over their bodies. The vines have the magical ability to stretch, almost similar to that of rubber. The most obvious feature of the aimian, however, is their gaping maw protected by purple barbs. These barbs do not interlock with each other but form a perfect barrier when the mouth is closed. They bear many similarities to the leaves of Venus flytraps.

    Habitat: Aimians live in jungles and deep within forests. They can even be found in areas with thick foliage that block most or all light onto the forest floor.

    Behavior: These creatures are very ambulatory, using their roots to move around and their sensitive vines to perceive the surrounding area since they are completely blind and possess limited auditory capabilities. Aimians are almost always on the move in search of food. When at rest, they often dig their pointed roots into the ground to soak up nutrients from the earth and sun. Nevertheless, water and sunlight are not required by aimians as they are particularly carnivorous and thrive on meat. They are even anthro-eating creatures, giving way to their high threat rating. Aimians hunt by entangling their prey in their vines and forcing them into their huge mouths either to pierce them on their sharp toothlike barbs or to trap and crush them inside. Once inside their mouths, prey is then liquified and consumed.

    Interactions: Aimians are easily avoided as they live in areas where most people do not travel. However, their vines are sought after as a higher-tension replacement for rubber.
