    Name: Ekzon
    Pronunciation: ek-ZAWN
    Scientific Name: Drazomagnus caballinus
    Classification: Feral
    Height: 2-4' (0.6-1.2 m)
    Threat Level: Low
    Associated Elements: None

    Etymology: From equine, horse-like, and the Common ending -zon meaning from the drazon family.

    Characteristics: Ekzons vary in size, coloration, markings, build, and speed depending on the breed of the individual creature. However, all breeds carry many similar characteristics. They are all scaly creatures that stand on all fours. All four feet have four digits, each with long, sharp claws. The undersides of their feet have thick, black, rubbery pads that protect them from rough terrain. All ekzons also have long, whip-like tails. Horns or spines grow from the backs of their heads based on the breed of ekzon.

    Habitat: Wild ekzons live in the plains regions all over Casarok. Domesticated ekzons are common in all parts of the world.

    Behavior: Ekzons are often ill-tempered creatures. The level of the temper they exhibit is based on the breed though. Some breeds are extremely temperamental while others can be more mild-mannered. These are usually split into "hot blooded" and "cold blooded" categories by breeders and tamers. Ekzons are known for their running capabilities. Wild ekzons, in fact, are known to challenge each other in feats of speed, either as a show of superiority or as a way of attracting a potential mate. Although they have large bulk and ample defense with their claws, they will rarely fight and instead prefer to run from danger. Ekzons are omnivorous. In the wild, they eat a wide variety of plants. They also chase down and trample small game when it comes to eating meat. In captivity they are fed specialized diets that are mostly made up of fruits and vegetables.

    Interactions: Domesticated ekzons are used for a wide variety of tasks. Throughout history they have been used for agricultural means. In earlier times before the invention of locomotives and automobiles, they were the primary means of ground transportation due to their speed and stamina. In modern times, they are used for sport. Equestrian is a popular pastime among the wealthy, who have the money capable of housing and feeding these large creatures. Ekzon racing is another common recreational activity. Some Anthros partake in gambling on races. Regardless of the activity they are involved in, it is common practice to cut away or file down the spines or horns that grow on their heads to avoid risk of injury to the rider. Some ekzon owners also fashion special shoes or magic shells for their feet to help protect them from wear.
