    Name: Humbaba
    Pronunciation: HOOM-bah-bah
    Scientific Name: Chimeravera humbaba
    Classification: Monster
    Height: 7-8' (2-2.5 m)
    Threat Level: Very High
    Associated Elements:

    Etymology: From the ancient Dašungallï language meaning "fire terror".

    Characteristics: Humbaba are similar to bipedal lions except they for their dark-colored thorn-like fur. The most common color for this creature is a dark greyish-brown. A thick mane covers their head and upper back, usually of a lighter hue than the rest of their fur. All humbaba have a scaly tail like that of a snake. Some subspecies of humbaba have horns on their heads or birdlike feet with talons. Regardless, all of these creatures have very sharp claws on their hands and feet. They are all capable of breathing fire.

    Habitat: Humbabas live in rocky areas, usually mountains and cliffsides. They are known to venture toward forests to hunt, although they do not use their fire breath when hunting. Forest fires started by humbabas are extremely rare.

    Behavior: Humbabas are solitary, extremely aggressive, and territorial. These creatures are considered one of the greatest singular threats in all of Casarok. They will unflinchingly assault any tresspasser upon sensing them on their land, even going so far as to try to repel others with their fire breath. As far as diet is concerned, they are strictly carnivorous and will actively hunt on and around their territory as well. Fights among humbabas are known to occur in territorial disputes and for selecting potential mates.

    Interactions: Humbabas provide no useful resources. They are often hunted out of necessity to ensure the safety of people in surrounding areas. In other cases, they are hunted for pure sport. Tusks, claws, and heads are often kept as trophies for successfully defeating such dangerous creatures.
