    Magic in Casarok

    Magic has been a driving force in Casarok throughout its long history. Since the first days of the intelligent life, mortals have been able to wield magic either through inherently being born with it or learning it through rigorous study. The gods and goddesses of Casarok, on the other hand, have always been able to use magic.

    Magic is used by drawing from one's own mana, the life force that exists within all creatures and the very planet itself. By manipulating one's mana in various methods and quantities, a person can cast a plethora of spells. Mana is a limited resource, however, due to the fact that it draws from the very power that sustains life. One's mana is depleted when their life force is weakened to the point that drawing from it further would do damage to the user. Mana can be restored by resting, eating, drinking, or consuming special products like mana potions that boost one's life force.

    Wielding magic can be dangerous to the user under certain circumstances. The most common magical affliction is known as mana sickness. If one continues to use magic beyond the zero-mana threshold previously stated, then they become susceptible to this disease the more they continue to cast spells. Symptoms include coughing, paleness of the skin or fur, and chills. Extreme cases can lead to permanent maximum mana reduction, muscular atrophy, coma, or even death. On the opposite end of the spectrum, one can have mana addiction. This affliction is noted by the physiological need to imbibe mana potions constantly. Mana addiction can be developed by fluctuations in life force such as consistently using and restoring mana. Symptoms are very similar to that of an alcohol or drug addiction. Despite the possible dangers, most magic users only experience slightly accelerated aging over their lifetime from tapping into their life force.

    Guide to the Elements

    • Fire
      Fire magic generally utilizes flames and heat to deal damage to enemies. Spells like Fireball and Heat Wave are common for this task. Some spells are capable of inflicting burns as well. Other spells, like Flamebrand, are capable of imbuing weapons with fire magic, adding previously mentioned spell effects or damage to it. Rarely, fire spells will grant some form of assistance. For example, Cauterize closes wounds to prevent someone from bleeding out. Masters of the fire element are known as pyromancers.
    • Frost
      Frost magic calls upon ice, snow, and cold for its spells. Common damage-inducing spells include Frostbolt and Cold Snap. Some spells like Freeze will instead encase someone in ice, immobilizing them or preserving them from damage. Frost magic is also capable of slowing movement, shrouding someone in a fog, or summon a blizzard to reduce enemy visibility. As for weapon-based magic, weapons like spears and maces can be created from ice. Users of the frost element are known as cryomancers.
    • Lightning
      Lightning magic is denoted by the use of sparks, lightning, and storms. Damage dealing spells like Lightning Bolt and Storm Surge are a key part of a thunder mage's repertoire. Some lightning spells are capable of inflicting paralyzing or stunning effects on a target like Shock. Area of effect spells are also a staple of lightning magic. Thunderstorm and Spark Shower are capable of spreading electricity throughout an area. Weapon-based spells tend to make the weapon lighter through magnetic force or paralyze the foe upon strike. Lightning magic users are known as electromancers.
    • Space (Arcane)
      In the School of the Arcane, space magic is fairly limited. The most common spells in this class are for creating portals, teleportation, far sight, spatial awareness, and summoning. Objects can also be created from pure mana, but that mana becomes unusable until the conjured object is dismissed. Some damage dealing attacks exist as well, usually through the manipulation of gravity as in Gravity Bolt or the power of stars like Starfire. Arcane space mages are known as conjurers and summoners.
    • Earth
      Users of earth magic are known as geomancers.
    • Water
      Water elementalists are better known as hydromancers.
    • Air
      Masters of air magic are known as aeromancers.
    • Time (Elemental)
      Elementalists specializing in time magic are known as scryers and seers.
    • Light (Metaphysical)
      Wielders of light magic are known as clerics.
    • Dark (Metaphysical)
      Masters of the dark arts are called sorcerers.
    • Nature
      Users of natural magics are called druids.
    • Spirit
      Spirit magic users are known as spiritualists.

    • Earth
      • Stoneshard: Fires a large piece of stone at a target
      • Petrify: Turns the target to stone, immobilizing them and making them susceptible to certain attacks
      • Stoneskin: Turns the target into living stone, acting like natural armor
    • Water
      • Waterblast: Fires a globe of water at a target
      • Fog: Summon a shroud around the user, reducing visibility or providing a smokescreen with which to escape
      • Healing Rain: Restores health to the target
    • Air
      • Windshear: Fires a gust of wind at a target
      • Mirage: Bends the air around a target to create false copies of them, decreasing the likelihood they will be hit with an attack
      • Hover: Causes the target to float in the air, either trapping them in place or allowing them to fly freely
    • Elemental Time
      • Warpblast: Fires a blast of temporally altered magic energy at a target
      • Slow: Forces the target to move in slow motion, reducing their movement, attacking, and casting speeds
      • Quicken: Causes the target to move in fast motion, increasing their movement, attacking, and casting speeds
    • Metaphysical Light
      • Lightbeam: Fires a blast of light energy at a target
      • Healing Light: Restores health to the target
      • Exorcise: Deals great damage to undead and unholy targets
    • Metaphysical Dark
      • Shadowbeam: Fires a blast of dark energy at a target
      • Life Drain: Steals health from one target and restores it to another
      • Shroud: Engulfs the target in shadows, reducing their visibility
    • Nature
      • Wrathblast: Fires a blast of natural energy at a target
      • Rejuvenate: Restores health to the target gradually over a duration
      • Envenom: Poisons the target, damaging them over time
    • Spirit
      • Soulfire: Fires a blast of spirit energy at a target
      • Spirit Mending: Restores health to the target gradually through channeling
      • Spirit Link: Splits damage and healing between the user and target
